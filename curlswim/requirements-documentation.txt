2.  Standard Components of a ConOps
A document following the IEEE structure typically includes the following sections:

Scope: Identifies the system and the document's purpose.

Referenced Documents: Any external standards or background materials.

Current System/Situation: Describes the "As-Is" state, including current problems or manual processes.

Justification for Change: Why the new system is needed (e.g., efficiency, new capabilities).

Proposed System: The "To-Be" state, describing high-level capabilities and the operational environment.

Operational Scenarios: Step-by-step "narratives" of how a user would interact with the system to complete a task.

Summary of Impacts: How the new system will change the organization, such as staffing or training needs.

Analysis: Trade-offs considered and why the chosen approach was selected.
